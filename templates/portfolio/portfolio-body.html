Echo

A mobile application to centre relevant university-related information such as: professors, classrooms, weekly schedule, enrollment certificate, cafeteria menu and meal tickets.


Sometimes, download your enrollment certificate, access your academic record, or maybe get to know what will be made for lunch on the cafeteria, for example, are some activities with great significance for a student. For a Unifesp student, by the time Echo was planned, these information were quite hard to be obtained; some could be found the university intranet, other on the university website, other even on the enrollment renewal website.


The student had to access different sources to get the information, added to the fact that the websites were not fully responsive, worsening the students' experience even more; as most of them use the cellphone to achieve these tasks.


Identifying the problem, and knowing there was a possibility to develop something to improve this situation, during the Human-Computer Interaction course, we deepened our knowledge about the problem, following the double diamond design process. Our first step was to discover, to see if this is a real problem faced by the students; and for that we chose some suitable research techniques for the situation: in-depth interview and card sorting.

Rango

Problem: 41k tons/year of food are wasted in Brazil. 15% are from restaurants: about 6 tons.

Hypothesis: throw food away = throw $$$$$ away.

Opportunity: create a solution that transforms the wastes into opportunity, for both the sellers and the customers.


Research: we made some in-depth interviews, with 7 owners of small establishments (bakery, markets, caf√©s), and 5 people that usually buys food through apps.


What we learned from the sellers:

    - All of them have a good control over their products;

    - The impact of the waste is about 3% of their budget;

    - They don't lose much, but have daily losses;

    - Care about the problem, and already try to deal with it through promotions or reusing on other recipes;

    - Prefere a manual way of management instead of softwares;


What we learned from the customers:

    - Most of them let the food spoil on their home;

    - Most of them uses iFood at least once a week, usually junkfood;

    - Care about the waste, but don't know what to do to solve the problem;

    - Would by food close to the expiration date, if cheap;

    - Are afraid of food poisoning;

    - Knowing the establishment would be a great motivator.


How might we...

    - Make the process of selling so easy, so that the seller won't feel tired or afraid of using the solution?

    - Help make the business grow?

    - Go through the fear of getting a food poisoning?

    - Increase the confidence over the platform?

    - Offer a good food for a good price?


Brand archetype: rango is an exporer. The users access our platform looking for opportunities, either to sell or to buy, and they do it exploring a totally new way of buying foods at the same they are contributing to a cause.


Usability test:

    - Most of them were afraid of the food being old, but would experiment because of the price;

    - Found it very easy to use;

    - Said the idea was innovative;

    - Got a little confused about the price;

    - Thought there were too little information about the boxes we were going to use to sell (size, how much food, ...);

    - Were in doubt about having delivery or not.


Post usability test enhancements:

    - Onboarding much leaner and more focused on the curation of the restaurants;

    - "Rescue a box" changed to "Withdraw a box" throughout the flow, to reinforce the fact that the person has to go and get the box.

    - Price being shown more clearly on the product page, in case the person hasn't read the onboarding, or simply didn't remember.

Drawing Identifier

As the name says... a drawing identifier. It was achieved using artificial intelligence.


A university project to apply a multilayer perceptron network onto an algorithm, so that it could learn to identify the classes of some images (used on the training process). When an image is provided for it to judge, it will be told the probability of this image to be each of the trained classes. The dataset was collected from Google Quick, Draw!
